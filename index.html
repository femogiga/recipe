Classic Cheesecake Recipe

Look no further for a creamy and ultra smooth classic cheesecake recipe! Paired with a buttery graham cracker crust,no one can deny its simple decadence/For the best results, bake in a water bath.

Ingredients

Graham Cracker Crust

1 and 1/2 cups(150g) graham cracker crumbs (about 10 full sheet graham crackers

5 Tablespoons (70g) unsalted butter, melted

1/4 cups (50g) granulated sugar

YIELDS

12 servings

PREP TIME

45 minutes

COOK TIME

1 hour

TOTAL TIME

7,75 hours

Cheesecake

four 8-ounce block(904g) full-fat cream cheese softened to room temperature

1 cups(200g) granulated sugar

1 cups(240g) full-fat sour cream at room temperature

1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract

2 teaspoons fresh lemon juice(optional, but recommended)

3 large eggs at room temperature

topping suggestions: salted caramel, lemon curd,strawberry topping,chocolate ganache, fresh fruit, whipped cream, or raspberry sauce(recipe in notes)

Instructions

  1. 1

    Adjust the oven rack to the lower-middle position and preheat oven to 350°F (177°C)

  2. 2

    Make the crust: Using a food processor, pulse the graham cracker into crumbs Pour into a medium bowl and stir in sugar and melted butter until combined. (you can also pulse it all together into the food processor.) Mixture will be sandy. Press firmly into bottom and and slightly up the side of a 9-inch or 10-inch springform pan. No need to grease the pan first. i use the bottom of a messuring cup to pack the crust down tightly. Pre-bake for 8 minutes. Remove from the oven and place the hot pan on a large piece of aluminium foil. the foil will wrap around the pan for the water bath in step 4. Allow crust to slightly cool as you prepare the filling

  3. 3

    Making the filling Using a handheld or stand mixer fitted with a paddle attachment, beat the cream cheese and granulated sugar together on medium-high speed in a large bowl until the mixture is smooth and creamy, about 2 minutes. Add the sour cream, vanilla extract, and lemon juice then beat until fully combined. On medium speed, add the eggs one at a time, beating after each addition until just blended.After the final egg is incorporated Instructionsthe batter, stop mixing.To help prevent the cheese from deflating and cracking as it cools, avoid over-mixing the batter as best as you can

  4. 4

    Prepare the simple water bath(see note) Boil a pot of water. You need 1 inch of water in your roasting pan for the water bath,so make sure you boil enough. I use an entire kettle of hot water. As the water is heating up, wrap the aluminium foil around the springform pan. Pour the cheesecake batter on top of the crust. use a rubber spatula or spoon to smooth it into and even layer. place the pan inside of the pan and place in the oven. (Or you can place the roasting pan in the oven firat, then pour the hot water in. Whichever is easier for you.)

  5. 5

    Bake cheesecake for 55-70 minutes or until the center is almost set. When it's done,the center of the cheesecake will slightly wobble if you gently shake the pan. Turn the oven off and open the oven door slightly. Let the cheesecake sit in the oven in the water bath as it cools down for 1 hour. remove from the oven and water bath, the n cool cheesecake completely at room temperature. Then refrigerate the cheesecake for at least 4 hours or overnight.

  6. 6

    Use a knife to loosen the chilled cheesecake from the rim of the springform pan, then remove the rim. Using a clean sharp knife, cut into the slices for serving.For neat slices, wipe the knife clean and dip into warm water between each slice.

  7. 7

    Serve cheesecake with desired toppings. Cover and store leftover cheesecake in the refrigerator for up to 5 days.
